# Social Media Checklist for College Students

 Do's
-  Think before posting – consider how it reflects on you.
-  Use privacy settings to control who sees your content.
-  Cite sources when sharing academic or professional information.
-  Engage respectfully in discussions and debates.
-  Report inappropriate or harmful content.

 Don'ts
-  Share personal information like addresses or phone numbers publicly.
-  Post when angry or upset – avoid impulsive communication.
-  Spread rumors, misinformation, or unverified news.
-  Bully, harass, or demean others online.
-  Overshare details that could harm your reputation or future opportunities.

- Poor digital communication can cause serious misunderstandings. For instance, a college student once posted a sarcastic comment about a professor on a public group chat, intending it as a joke. However, the message was misinterpreted as disrespectful and spread quickly among peers. The professor eventually saw it, leading to disciplinary action and damaged trust between the student and faculty. This situation could have been avoided if the student had paused to consider tone and audience before posting. Using private channels for casual remarks and maintaining professionalism in public forums are better practices. Responsible digital communication means being mindful of context, clarity, and respect, which helps prevent unnecessary conflicts and protects one’s reputation.

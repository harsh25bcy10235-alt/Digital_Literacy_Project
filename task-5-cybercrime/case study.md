Case Study: Phishing Attack on a University Employee
Phishing is one of the cyber crimes in which the attacker sends an email to the victim by assuming the identity of the sender and convinces the victim to reveal personal information such as the password, bank details, etc. The most common phishing attack occurs when the attacker uses the technique of combining social engineering and fraudulent electronic communication to deceive the victim’s trust.

In the above scenario, the administrative assistant of the university, Riya Sharma, receives an email from the attacker in the name of the IT department of the university. The email sends the victim into a panic because it says that the email account of Riya Sharma will be deactivated if she does not immediately verify her email ID and password.

Step-by-Step Progression of the Attack:

The attacker sends an email with the email ID similar to the university’s email ID.
The attacker sends the email with the subject line that says, “Your email ID and password verification is mandatory.”
The attacker sends the email with the message that says, “Your account will be locked in 24 hours.” This is done to scare the victim.
The attacker sends the email with the link that says it is the email ID and password verification page.
The victim believes that the email is genuine and enters the email ID and password.
The attacker receives the email ID and password immediately and gains unauthorized access to the university’s internal systems.

The victims of the phishing attack are the students, employees, customers of financial institutions, and people who make frequent use of electronic communication.

The consequences of the phishing attack in this scenario were severe because the attacker was able to access the confidential details of the students, redirect the financial reimbursement process, and even shut down some of the university’s systems. Riya’s email ID was locked, all the passwords were reset, and the investigation was carried out.
